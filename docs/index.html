Identifying Objects at Boundaries

Towards Advancing Educational Research Collaboration Across Disciplines

Authors
Affiliation

Faculty of Teacher Training and Education, Sanata Dharma University

Faculty of Teacher Training and Education, Sanata Dharma University

Published

January 2, 2025

The purpose of this report is to identify common topics in undergraduate and master’s theses across departments within the Faculty of Teacher Training and Education at Sanata Dharma University. To achieve this, we conducted a bibliometric analysis of theses from the years 2022 and 2023. The analysis utilized data consisting of thesis titles, which were subsequently coded to generate a list of keywords for each thesis.

Our analyses yielded three key insights. First, we examined the frequency of keywords across departments, providing an overview of common topics within the faculty and within individual departments. Second, we analyzed the connections between keywords through a network perspective, offering insights into how topics are interrelated and identifying clusters of related topics. Third, we explored the connections between keywords and departments to assess the extent of interdepartmental linkages.

3 Interdepartmental Linkages Based on Keywords

Figure 4 displays the connections between departments and keywords, illustrating how different academic disciplines relate to various research topics. The size of the nodes represents the weighted degree, indicating the strength of each node’s connections. The color of the nodes differentiates the types of nodes: departments are represented in green, while keywords are represented in pink, visually distinguishing the two types of entities in the network.

The table below presents the degree, weighted degree, and betweenness centrality of each node in the bipartite network. The degree indicates the number of direct connections each node has, while the weighted degree adjusts this count to account for the strength of the connections. Betweenness centrality reflects the extent to which a node serves as a bridge between other nodes, highlighting its role in facilitating connections across the network.

The table below shows each keyword along with the number of departments it is associated with and the list of departments. This provides insight into the extent to which certain keywords are shared across different departments.

Figure 5 illustrates the connections between departments based on the shared keywords in their theses. Each link between two departments represents a shared keyword, and the thickness of the link indicates the strength of the connection, with thicker links showing a higher number of shared keywords. This visualization highlights how departments are interconnected through common research topics, helping to identify areas of overlap and potential collaboration within the faculty.

Figure 6 offers a detailed representation of the connections between departments, complementing the chord diagram in Figure 5. While the chord diagram emphasizes the overall network structure and connection strength, the heatmap highlights the exact weights of shared keywords between department pairs. Each tile corresponds to a department pair, with darker shades indicating stronger connections. Numerical values within the tiles provide precise weights, facilitating a closer examination of thematic linkages across departments.
